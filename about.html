About Coach Eli

Player development isn’t just about the work done inside the lines—it’s about preparing athletes for the moments that define them.

My approach is rooted in collegiate-level preparation. Serving as a Student Assistant Coach for the Slippery Rock University NCAA D2 Women’s Basketball team, alongside my experience building players at the varsity high school and Hoop Group Scholastic League (HGSL) levels, has shown me exactly what the next tier demands. I don't run stationary, fluff drills, and I don't waste time on empty repetitions that look good on camera but fail in a real game.

Every environment I build is engineered to push your physical and mental limits, slow down the geometry of the floor, and elevate your situational basketball IQ under extreme defensive pressure. We train at game speed, with real constraints, so that decision-making becomes second nature when the game is on the line.

But dominant on-court performance is only half the battle. Basketball is our vehicle to teach critical life lessons. Through intense preparation, strict accountability, and mental toughness, we don't just build better players—we build the competitive character required for athletes to win decisively in life, long after the final buzzer sounds.
